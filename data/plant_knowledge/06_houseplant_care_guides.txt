Plant: Monstera deliciosa
Common Name: Swiss Cheese Plant
Light: Bright indirect light. Tolerates some direct morning sun. Avoid harsh afternoon sun which can scorch leaves. Leaves will not develop fenestrations (holes) without adequate light.
Water: Water when the top 2 inches of soil are dry. Typically every 7-10 days in summer, 14-21 days in winter. Prefers consistently moist but never waterlogged soil.
Humidity: Prefers 60%+ humidity. Benefits from humidifier or pebble tray. Will tolerate lower humidity but may develop crispy edges.
Soil: Well-draining aroid mix — 1 part potting soil, 1 part orchid bark, 1 part perlite.
Temperature: 65-85°F (18-29°C). Avoid below 55°F.
Fertilizer: Feed monthly during spring and summer with balanced liquid fertilizer diluted to half strength. Stop feeding in winter.
Common Issues: Root rot from overwatering, brown edges from low humidity, yellow leaves from overwatering, no fenestrations from low light.
Support: Provide a moss pole or trellis — Monstera is a climbing plant and produces larger leaves with support.

Plant: Pothos (Epipremnum aureum)
Common Name: Devil's Ivy, Golden Pothos
Light: Extremely adaptable — tolerates low light to bright indirect. Variegated varieties need more light to maintain variegation. Avoid direct sun which can burn leaves.
Water: Allow top inch of soil to dry between waterings. Very forgiving of missed waterings. Leaves will droop when thirsty — a reliable signal.
Humidity: Adaptable to normal household humidity (30-60%). Thrives in higher humidity but doesn't require it.
Soil: Standard well-draining potting mix. Not picky about soil.
Temperature: 60-85°F (15-29°C). Avoid below 50°F.
Fertilizer: Feed every 2-4 weeks during growing season with diluted balanced fertilizer. Reduce in winter.
Common Issues: Root rot if chronically overwatered, leggy growth in low light, loss of variegation in insufficient light, brown tips from underwatering.
Propagation: Extremely easy — cut below a node and root in water. Roots appear in 1-2 weeks.

Plant: Fiddle Leaf Fig (Ficus lyrata)
Common Name: Fiddle Leaf Fig
Light: Bright indirect light, ideally from a south or west-facing window. Some direct morning sun is beneficial. Consistent light is important — do not rotate frequently.
Water: Water when top inch is dry. Use room-temperature water. Sensitive to both overwatering and underwatering. Establish a consistent weekly schedule and adjust seasonally.
Humidity: Prefers 30-65%. Can tolerate average household humidity. Very dry air may cause brown edges.
Soil: Well-draining potting mix with perlite. Does not tolerate sitting in water.
Temperature: 60-75°F (15-24°C). Very sensitive to cold drafts and temperature fluctuations.
Fertilizer: Feed monthly spring through summer with fiddle leaf fig-specific or 3-1-2 NPK ratio fertilizer.
Common Issues: Brown spots (bacterial or fungal — diagnose by location and pattern), leaf drop from stress, edema from inconsistent watering, root rot from overwatering. Most problems come from inconsistency.
Tip: Pick a spot and leave it there. Fiddle leaf figs hate being moved.

Plant: Snake Plant (Sansevieria trifasciata)
Common Name: Snake Plant, Mother-in-Law's Tongue
Light: One of the most adaptable houseplants — thrives from low light to bright indirect. Can handle some direct sun. Growth is faster in brighter light.
Water: Water every 2-6 weeks depending on light and season. Let soil dry COMPLETELY between waterings. The single most important rule — when in doubt, don't water.
Humidity: Very adaptable. Tolerates dry air, heating vents, and air conditioning better than most plants.
Soil: Fast-draining cactus or succulent mix. Heavy soil that stays wet will kill it.
Temperature: 60-85°F (15-29°C). Can tolerate brief periods down to 50°F but extended cold damages leaves.
Fertilizer: Feed sparingly — once or twice during growing season with diluted cactus fertilizer. Overfertilizing is more harmful than underfertilizing.
Common Issues: Root rot from overwatering (by far the #1 killer), mushy leaves, brown tips from cold or inconsistent watering. Extremely hard to kill otherwise.
Air Purifying: NASA study confirmed snake plants remove formaldehyde and benzene from air.

Plant: Peace Lily (Spathiphyllum)
Common Name: Peace Lily
Light: Low to medium indirect light. One of the best flowering houseplants for low light. Will bloom more in brighter indirect light. Avoid direct sun.
Water: Keep soil consistently moist but not soggy. Dramatic drooping when thirsty is a helpful signal — the plant recovers fully after watering. Water with room-temperature filtered water.
Humidity: Prefers 50%+ humidity. Benefits from misting or humidifier. Dry air causes brown tips.
Soil: Rich, well-draining potting mix. Slightly moisture-retentive is fine.
Temperature: 65-80°F (18-27°C). Sensitive to cold below 55°F.
Fertilizer: Feed every 6-8 weeks during growing season with diluted balanced fertilizer. Overfertilizing causes brown leaf tips.
Common Issues: Brown tips from dry air, minerals in water, or fertilizer. Yellow leaves from overwatering. Failure to bloom from insufficient light. Mildly toxic to pets if ingested.

Plant: Chinese Evergreen (Aglaonema)
Common Name: Chinese Evergreen
Light: Low to medium indirect light. More colorful varieties (red, pink) need brighter light to maintain color. Solid green varieties tolerate the lowest light.
Water: Water when top 1-2 inches of soil are dry. Moderate water needs. Does not like waterlogged soil. Reduce watering in winter.
Humidity: Tolerates normal humidity but prefers 40-60%. Brown tips indicate low humidity.
Soil: Well-draining standard potting mix. Add perlite if mix is heavy.
Temperature: 65-80°F (18-27°C). Very sensitive to cold — keep above 60°F. Avoid cold drafts.
Fertilizer: Feed monthly during growing season with balanced liquid fertilizer at half strength.
Common Issues: Yellow leaves from overwatering, brown tips from cold or dry air, loss of color variegation in low light. Generally very resilient.

Plant: Spider Plant (Chlorophytum comosum)
Common Name: Spider Plant, Airplane Plant
Light: Bright indirect light preferred. Tolerates low light but grows slower and may not produce spiderettes. Avoid direct sun which can bleach leaves.
Water: Water when top inch of soil is dry. Fleshy roots store water — fairly drought tolerant. Very sensitive to fluoride and chlorine in tap water — use filtered water.
Humidity: Adaptable to household humidity. Higher humidity promotes spiderette production.
Soil: Standard well-draining potting mix.
Temperature: 55-80°F (13-27°C). Fairly cold-tolerant for a houseplant.
Fertilizer: Feed monthly during spring and summer with diluted balanced fertilizer. Excess fertilizer causes brown tips.
Common Issues: Brown tips from fluoride in water, chemicals in tap water, or overfertilizing. Fading leaf color from too much direct sun. Root bound quickly — repot when roots fill the pot or grow out the drainage holes.

Plant: Rubber Plant (Ficus elastica)
Common Name: Rubber Tree, Rubber Plant
Light: Bright indirect light. Tolerates medium light. Burgundy varieties need more light to maintain deep color. Direct morning sun is beneficial.
Water: Water when top 2 inches of soil are dry. Reduce watering significantly in winter. Overwatering causes leaf drop and root rot.
Humidity: Tolerates average household humidity. Wipe leaves regularly with damp cloth to remove dust and improve light absorption.
Soil: Well-draining mix with perlite. Standard potting mix with amendments works well.
Temperature: 60-80°F (15-27°C). Avoid cold drafts which cause sudden leaf drop.
Fertilizer: Feed monthly during growing season with balanced liquid fertilizer.
Common Issues: Leaf drop from environmental changes (drafts, relocation, temperature shifts), root rot from overwatering, leggy growth from low light.
Tip: The milky sap (latex) can irritate skin — wear gloves when pruning.

Plant: ZZ Plant (Zamioculcas zamiifolia)
Common Name: ZZ Plant, Zanzibar Gem
Light: Extremely low-light tolerant. Grows in offices with only fluorescent lighting. Faster growth in bright indirect light. Tolerates neglect exceptionally well.
Water: Water every 2-4 weeks. The rhizomes (thick underground stems) store significant water. Allow soil to dry completely between waterings. This plant thrives on neglect.
Humidity: Very adaptable to any household humidity level.
Soil: Well-draining mix. Cactus mix works well. Does not tolerate wet feet.
Temperature: 60-85°F (15-29°C). Tolerates a wide range.
Fertilizer: Feed 2-3 times per year with diluted balanced fertilizer during growing season. Very light feeder.
Common Issues: Yellow stems from overwatering (the most common problem), slow growth in very low light (but still survives). One of the hardest houseplants to kill.
Note: Mildly toxic if ingested. Wash hands after handling.

Plant: Aloe Vera
Common Name: Aloe Vera, Burn Plant
Light: Bright direct to indirect light. A south or west-facing window is ideal. Can sunburn if moved from low light to intense sun without acclimation.
Water: Soak-and-dry method — water deeply, then let soil dry completely (every 2-3 weeks in summer, monthly in winter). Leaves store water — very drought tolerant.
Humidity: Prefers dry air. High humidity increases rot risk.
Soil: Fast-draining cactus or succulent mix. Add extra perlite or coarse sand for drainage.
Temperature: 55-80°F (13-27°C). Can tolerate brief cool periods but not frost.
Fertilizer: Feed once in spring and once in summer with diluted cactus fertilizer. Very light feeder.
Common Issues: Mushy translucent leaves from overwatering, brown dried leaves from underwatering, leggy stretched growth from insufficient light, leaf tip browning from minerals in water.
Medicinal Use: Gel inside leaves is used for minor burns and skin irritation. Only use fresh gel from mature outer leaves.

Plant: Jade Plant (Crassula ovata)
Common Name: Jade Plant, Money Tree
Light: Bright light with some direct sun. 4-6 hours of direct sun produces best growth and compact form. Insufficient light causes leggy stretching.
Water: Water when soil is completely dry. Thick leaves and stems store water. Typically every 2-3 weeks in summer, monthly in winter. Reduce watering in cold months.
Humidity: Prefers dry to average humidity. Does not need misting.
Soil: Well-draining cactus or succulent mix.
Temperature: 55-75°F (13-24°C). Cool winter temperatures (55-60°F at night) may encourage blooming in mature plants.
Fertilizer: Feed every 2-3 months during growing season with diluted balanced or cactus fertilizer.
Common Issues: Leaf drop from overwatering, underwatering, or sudden temperature change. Wrinkled leaves from underwatering. Mushy stems from root rot. Leggy growth from low light.
Longevity: Can live for decades with proper care. Some jade plants are 50+ years old.

Plant: String of Pearls (Senecio rowleyanus)
Common Name: String of Pearls, String of Beads
Light: Bright indirect light. Some direct morning sun is beneficial. Avoid harsh afternoon sun. Insufficient light causes sparse strands and smaller pearls.
Water: Water when soil is completely dry. The pearl-shaped leaves store water. Very susceptible to overwatering — more strings of pearls die from overwatering than any other cause.
Humidity: Prefers dry to average humidity. Good air circulation is important.
Soil: Very fast-draining succulent mix with extra perlite. Some growers use mostly mineral substrate.
Temperature: 55-80°F (13-27°C). Brief cool periods are tolerated.
Fertilizer: Feed monthly during spring and summer with diluted succulent fertilizer. Stop in winter.
Common Issues: Shriveling pearls from underwatering, mushy pearls from overwatering, sparse growth from low light, dying back from the crown (usually overwatering).
Watering Tip: Water from the bottom to keep the crown dry and prevent rot.

Plant: Bird of Paradise (Strelitzia)
Common Name: Bird of Paradise
Light: Bright direct to indirect light. Needs 4-6 hours of direct sun indoors to potentially flower. Will survive in medium light but won't bloom and growth will be slow.
Water: Water when top 2 inches of soil are dry. Moderate water needs. Likes consistent moisture during growing season, reduced watering in winter.
Humidity: Prefers 50-60% humidity. Benefits from regular misting or humidifier, especially in winter.
Soil: Rich, well-draining potting mix with perlite.
Temperature: 65-80°F (18-27°C). Can tolerate down to 50°F briefly. No frost tolerance.
Fertilizer: Feed every 2 weeks during growing season with balanced fertilizer. Hungry plant that benefits from regular feeding.
Common Issues: Leaf splitting (natural — helps with wind resistance in nature), curling leaves from underwatering or low humidity, yellow lower leaves from natural aging or overwatering. Indoor flowering is rare — requires years of maturity and abundant light.

Plant: Calathea (various species)
Common Name: Prayer Plant, Calathea, Goeppertia
Light: Medium indirect light. Avoid direct sun which fades and burns the decorative leaf patterns. Too little light causes patterns to fade differently — leaves become uniformly dark.
Water: Keep soil consistently lightly moist. Do not let soil dry out completely. Use filtered, distilled, or rainwater only — very sensitive to tap water chemicals.
Humidity: Requires 50%+ humidity minimum. 60-70% ideal. Will develop crispy edges below 40%. Humidifier is strongly recommended.
Soil: Well-draining but moisture-retentive mix. Standard potting soil with added perlite and peat.
Temperature: 65-80°F (18-27°C). Avoid temperatures below 60°F. No cold drafts.
Fertilizer: Feed monthly during growing season with diluted balanced fertilizer at quarter to half strength. Sensitive to fertilizer burn.
Common Issues: Crispy brown edges from low humidity or tap water, leaf curling from underwatering, fading patterns from too much or too little light, spider mites (check undersides regularly). High-maintenance but rewarding.
